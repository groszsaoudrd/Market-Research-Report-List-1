<p><p>Network sandboxing software is categorized into two primary market types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions. Cloud-based sandboxing leverages remote servers to analyze and isolate potential threats, offering scalability, ease of deployment, and reduced hardware costs. In contrast, on-premises solutions are installed directly on a company's infrastructure, providing greater control and security, often favored by organizations with stringent data compliance requirements. Both types aim to enhance cybersecurity by enabling the safe testing of suspicious files and applications.</p></p>
