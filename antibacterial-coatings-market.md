<p><ul><li>Metallic Coatings</li><li>Non-Metallic Coatings</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The antibacterial coatings market is divided into two main types: metallic and non-metallic coatings. Metallic coatings, typically composed of materials like silver, copper, or zinc, exploit the antimicrobial properties of metals to inhibit bacterial growth on surfaces. Non-metallic coatings, on the other hand, utilize organic compounds or polymers infused with antibacterial agents such as quaternary ammonium compounds or chitosan, providing effective microbial resistance while often being more versatile for various applications. Together, these coatings enhance hygiene and safety across multiple industries.</p></p>
