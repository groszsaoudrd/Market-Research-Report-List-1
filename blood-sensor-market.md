<p><p>The blood sensor market is primarily categorized into minimally invasive and non-invasive types. Minimally invasive blood sensors involve small punctures or insertions, allowing for blood analysis with reduced discomfort. They often use devices like microneedles or microfluidics. In contrast, non-invasive blood sensors utilize technologies that measure blood parameters without skin penetration, relying on methods like spectroscopy or bioimpedance. Both types aim to enhance patient comfort and streamline monitoring processes in clinical and home settings.</p></p>
