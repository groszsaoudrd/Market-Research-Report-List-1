<p><p>Lignite mining involves extracting low-grade coal, primarily for energy production. The lignite market includes two main processing types: liquefaction and gasification. Liquefaction converts lignite into synthetic crude oil, enabling its use in various petroleum applications. Gasification, on the other hand, transforms lignite into syngas, which can be utilized for electricity generation, chemical production, or as a fuel. Both processes are crucial for enhancing lignite's value and reducing environmental impact, supporting the transition to cleaner energy sources.</p></p>
