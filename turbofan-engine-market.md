<p><p>The turbofan engine market comprises three primary types based on bypass ratio and application. Low-bypass turbofans are commonly used in military jets, offering high thrust and speed. Afterburning turbofans, utilized in supersonic aircraft, enhance thrust by burning additional fuel in the exhaust, ideal for combat and performance aircraft. High-bypass turbofans, prevalent in commercial aviation, provide excellent fuel efficiency and lower noise levels, making them suitable for passenger transport. Each type serves different performance and operational requirements.</p></p>
