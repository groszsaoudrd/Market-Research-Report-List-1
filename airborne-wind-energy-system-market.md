<p><p>Airborne Wind Energy Systems (AWES) can be categorized into two main market types: non-rotating and rotating systems. Non-rotating systems utilize tethered kites or drones that harness wind energy without conventional turbine blades, generating power while gliding through the air. In contrast, rotating systems resemble traditional wind turbines but are elevated to harness higher-altitude winds. Both categories aim to improve energy efficiency and reduce costs, appealing to different applications and deployment strategies in the renewable energy sector.</p></p>
