<p><p>Floor-standing laboratory centrifuges are classified into high-speed and low-speed types based on their operational capabilities. High-speed centrifuges can achieve rapid spinning speeds, typically over 15,000 RPM, facilitating the separation of fine particles and biological samples like proteins and nucleic acids. In contrast, low-speed centrifuges operate at lower RPMs, generally under 5,000 RPM, ideal for applications that require gentle separation, such as blood component separation and sedimentation of larger particles. Both play crucial roles in research and clinical laboratories.</p></p>
